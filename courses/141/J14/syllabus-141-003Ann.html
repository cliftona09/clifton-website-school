Learning Outcomes:

A student who successfully completes Calculus I (MATH 141) should be beginning to develop as an independent learner and problem solver with the ability to approach problems from a conceptual viewpoint and to apply appropriate calculus skills to problems in context.

In particular, the successful student will master concepts and gain skills needed to solve problems related to:

functions and graphs, finding limits graphically, numerically, and analytically, continuity and one-sided limits, infinite limits and limits at infinity, the derivative and rates of change, basic differentiation rules, extrema on an interval, Mean Value Theorem, derivatives and the shape of a graph, l’Hospital’s rule and indeterminate forms, optimization problems, antiderivatives and indefinite integrals, area and distance, definite integrals and The Fundamental Theorem of Calculus.
